Cookie Policy

When you visit our websites, we send one or more cookies – a small file containing a string of characters – to your computer or other device that uniquely identifies your browser. We use cookies to improve the quality of our service, including for storing user preferences, and tracking user trends.
What are cookies?
Cookies are small text files stored by your browser as you browse the internet. They can be used to collect, store, and share data about your activities across websites, including on CourseFrenzy. Cookies also allow us to remember things about your visits to CourseFrenzy, like your preferred language, and to make the site easier to use.
We use both session cookies, which expire after a short time or when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain stored in your browser for a set period of time. We use session cookies to identify you during a single browsing session, like when you log into CourseFrenzy. We use persistent cookies where we need to identify you over a longer period, like when you request that we keep you signed in.

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • To log you into CourseFrenzy.
  • To protect your security.
  • To help detect and fight spam, abuse, and other activities that violate CourseFrenzy agreements.
  • To remember data about your browser and your preferences.
  • To remember your settings and other choices you’ve made.
  • To help us improve and understand how people use CourseFrenzy.
